Top Tourist Destinations of Pakistan





Lahore

Tourists love Lahore since it is the second-largest city in Pakistan after Karachi. It is one of the most beautiful cities in Pakistan and is considered to be one of the most developed Pakistani cities with modern infrastructure. Lahore is known for its Mughal architecture, beautiful gardens, farmhouses, and historic buildings.

The vibrant culture and warm people make Lahore worth visiting. Also, Lahore has a very old and interesting history. Lahore is a major center of Qawwali music. The city also hosts much of Pakistan's tourist industry, with major attractions including the Walled City, the famous Badshahi and Wazir Khan mosques, as well as several Sikh and Sufi shrines

The old city of Lahore, surrounded by 16th-century walls, is a treasure trove of historical sites, bazaars, and narrow winding streets. Some highlights include the bustling bazaars of Anarkali and Delhi Gate, as well as the iconic Wazir Khan Mosque.







Karachi

Karachi is a popular tourist destination for domestic and international tourists. It is the capital of Sindh and the biggest metropolitan city in Pakistan. It is known for its diverse culture, rich cuisine, historical sites, advanced education system, various farmhouses, top cinemas, best shopping malls, and, of course, the beautiful deep blue sea.

Clifton Beach is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Karachi, a large crowd gathers here, especially on weekends and public holidays. However, there are also many other attractions in Karachi like Clifton Beach, Mazar-e-Quaid, Charna Island, Mohatta Palace, Haleji Lake, Frere Hall, Turtle Beach, and Port Grand, Karachi Zoo, Quaid e Azam Museum, Chaukhandi Tombs, Empress Market and many more.

Mazar-e-Quaid, Charna Island, Mohatta Palace, Haleji Lake, Frere Hall, Turtle Beach, and Port Grand, Karachi Zoo, Quaid e Azam Museum, Chaukhandi Tombs, Empress Market and many more.







Islamabad

As Pakistan’s capital and a modern metropolis. It is one of the country’s most visited cities. Islamabad is known for the presence of several parks and forests, including the Margalla Hills National Park and the Shakarparian. It is home to several landmarks, with the most notable one being the Faisal Mosque, the largest mosque in South Asia and the fifth-largest in the world.

Islamabad is the go-to tourist destination for both foreign as well as local travellers. Margalla Zoo, Daman-i-Koh, Pakistan Monument, Shakarparian, Lok Virsa Museum, and Rawal Lake viewpoint are among the top tourist attractions in Islamabad.

The Islamabad Zoo is home to a variety of animals and birds, making it a favorite spot for families. Visitors can enjoy observing wildlife and engaging in recreational activities within the zoo premises.







Multan

Multan, located in the province of Punjab, Pakistan, is a city rich in history, culture, and religious significance. It is often referred to as the "City of Saints" due to its numerous shrines and spiritual heritage.

A historical landmark, Multan Fort is one of the oldest surviving forts in Pakistan. It dates back to the ancient times of Alexander the Great and has seen various rulers over the centuries. The fort complex houses various monuments, including Qasim Bagh and the Tomb of Bahauddin Zakariya.

The shrine of the famous Sufi saint, Bahauddin Zakariya, is a significant religious site for Muslims. It attracts both devotees and tourists seeking spiritual solace.







Bahawalpur

The city has many hidden gem spots that will make you forget everything else. It is located right near the Cholistan Desert, so there are multiple attractions here that can appeal to everyone. Bahawalpur is surrounded by many tourist spots, like Lal Sohanra National Park and famous shrines like Uchh Sharif. However, Darbar Mahal is also one of the most stunning royal palaces in the city of Bahawalpur.

Noor Mahal is a stunning palace that was constructed in 1872. It combines Italian and Oriental architectural styles and served as the residence of the royal family. Visitors can explore the opulent interiors, lush gardens, and learn about the city's royal history.

The Bahawalpur Museum is a treasure trove of historical artifacts, including ancient coins, manuscripts, weapons, and artwork. It provides insights into the region's history, culture, and archaeological discoveries.







Gwadar

Gwadar, located in the southwestern province of Balochistan, Pakistan, is a rapidly developing port city that has gained significant attention in recent years. Golf enthusiasts can enjoy a round of golf at Gwadar Golf City, which features an 18-hole championship golf course. The lush greenery and scenic surroundings provide a unique golfing experience.

Gwadar boasts pristine and picturesque beaches along the Arabian Sea coastline. The serene and untouched beauty of the beaches, such as the famous Kund Malir Beach and Hammerhead Beach, attracts tourists who enjoy sunbathing, swimming, and relaxing amidst the natural surroundings.

As the centerpiece of the city, Gwadar Port is a deep-sea port that serves as a major hub for international trade and shipping. Visitors can witness the bustling activities of cargo ships, explore the port area, and learn about its strategic significance.







Peshawar

Peshawar, the capital city of the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province in Pakistan, is a city rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. It is known for its ancient roots, vibrant bazaars, and proximity to the famous Khyber Pass "Bab-e-Khyber" in Peshawar, it is a historic gateway that marks the entrance to the Khyber Pass. Bab-e-Khyber, also known as the "Gateway of Khyber," is a significant landmark in Peshawar and holds historical and cultural importance.

The city is home to a number of sites, including Bala Hisar Fort, Peshawar Museum, the archaeological site of Gor Khuttree, Mohabbat Khan Mosque, and the old city of Sethi Mohallah, Jamrud Fort, the Sphola Stupa, and the most famous market of Qissa Khawani. A trip to Peshawar simply isn’t complete without seeing the beautiful (and historical) Mahabat Khan Masjid. Located deep in the narrow twists and turns of the Old City, this 17th-century mosque reflects the architecture of the famed Mughal Era and remains in relatively good condition despite its advanced age.

Abbottabad is home to the Pakistan Military Academy (PMA), the prestigious institution where future officers of the Pakistani armed forces receive their training. The PMA offers guided tours for visitors, allowing them to witness the discipline and dedication of the cadets and learn about the military's role in Pakistan's history.

Abbottabad

Abbottabad, located in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan, is a picturesque city nestled in the beautiful Himalayan foothills. Known for its stunning natural landscapes, rich history, and pleasant weather, Abbottabad has become an increasingly popular tourist destination in recent years.

One of the main attractions in Abbottabad is its captivating scenery. Surrounded by lush green hills, Abbottabad offers breathtaking views and a serene environment. Visitors can enjoy the fresh mountain air, go for leisurely walks, or embark on hiking expeditions to nearby peaks such as Miranjani or Thandiani. These trails offer not only incredible panoramic views but also a chance to immerse oneself in the region's natural beauty

Abbottabad has its own unique charm. The city has several historical sites that provide insights into its past. The magnificent Abbottabad Clock Tower, a British-era landmark, stands tall in the heart of the city, serving as a reminder of its colonial heritage. Another notable attraction is the Ilyasi Mosque, which dates back to the Mughal period and showcases intricate architectural designs.







Swat

Swat, located in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan, is a breathtakingly beautiful valley that has gained popularity as a tourist destination in recent years. Known as the "Switzerland of the East," Swat offers a mesmerizing blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality

One of the main attractions of Swat is its stunning landscapes. The valley is surrounded by towering mountains, lush green meadows, and crystal-clear rivers. The picturesque beauty of Swat has earned it a reputation as one of the most scenic regions in Pakistan. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views, go for nature walks, or indulge in activities such as camping, trekking, and fishing.

For adventure enthusiasts, Swat provides ample opportunities for thrilling activities. The valley is home to several hiking trails, including the famous trek to Malam Jabba, a popular skiing destination in the winter months

Swat Valley in Pakistan is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered by tourists. Its breathtaking landscapes, historical sites, cultural richness, and adventure opportunities make it an attractive destination.







Chitral

Chitral is one of the most remarkable places in the world when it comes to natural scenic beauty, the simplicity of its residents, and its unique culture. Which is the most fascinating and charming place in the Hindu Kush range. Its picturesque mountains, juniper forests, beautiful springs, friendly people, and rivers teeming with the famous trout fish are truly spellbinding.

Chitral is also famous because of the Kalash Valley, which is a place of the Kalash people. People come here from all over the globe to enjoy their festivals like Jashn-e-Nowroz and Chilmijhust. Another very famous festival held in this region is the Shandur Polo festival. However, the weather in Chitral is extremely harsh and cold in winter, unlike in other parts of the country, while the summer is very pleasant and the best season to visit the area.

Chitral provides an unforgettable experience for travelers seeking an authentic and off-the-beaten-path destination in the stunning surroundings of the Hindu Kush mountains.







Muzaffarabad

Muzaffarabad, the capital city of Azad Jammu and Kashmir (AJK) in Pakistan, is a picturesque destination that offers a delightful blend of natural beauty, historical landmarks, and cultural experiences. Situated along the banks of the Jhelum River and surrounded by majestic mountains, Muzaffarabad attracts tourists from all over the country and beyond

Muzaffarabad is also known for its historical landmarks. The Red Fort (also known as the Muzaffarabad Fort) is a prominent attraction that dates back to the 16th century. This ancient fort offers a glimpse into the region's history and provides stunning views of the city and the river below. The nearby Chinar Bagh Park is a beautiful garden where visitors can relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

uzaffarabad in Pakistan offers a wonderful blend of natural beauty, historical landmarks, cultural experiences, and adventure opportunities. With its stunning landscapes, rich heritage, and warm hospitality, Muzaffarabad is an enchanting destination for tourists seeking a memorable and immersive experience in the heart of Azad Jammu and Kashmir.







Gilgit

Gilgit, located in the Gilgit-Baltistan region of Pakistan, is a breathtakingly beautiful destination known for its stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and adventurous opportunities. It serves as a gateway to some of the world's highest mountain ranges, making it a popular destination for tourists and adventurers alike.

Gilgit-Baltistan is home to all five of Pakistan's "eight-thousanders" and to more than fifty peaks above 7,000 meters (23,000 ft). Gilgit and Skardu are the two main hubs for expeditions to those mountains.

The Gilgit River, which flows through the city, adds to the scenic charm and provides opportunities for river rafting and other water-based activities. The majestic Attabad Lake, formed as a result of a landslide in 2010, is another must-visit attraction near Gilgit, offering turquoise waters and awe-inspiring views of the surrounding mountains.

Gilgit in Pakistan is a destination that promises an unforgettable experience for travelers seeking natural beauty, cultural immersion, and adventure. With its majestic mountains, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality, Gilgit offers a unique opportunity to explore the wonders of the Karakoram region and create lasting memories of a lifetime.







Murree

Murree, often referred to as the "Queen of Hills," is a popular tourist destination in Pakistan. Located in the Rawalpindi District of Punjab province, it is known for its pleasant weather, beautiful landscapes, and charming atmosphere. Murree offers a delightful retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life, making it a favored getaway for locals and tourists alike.

One of the main attractions of Murree is its refreshing climate. Situated at an altitude of approximately 2,291 meters (7,516 feet), the town enjoys cool summers and snowy winters, making it a perfect escape from the scorching heat of the plains. The cool mountain air, lush greenery, and misty surroundings create a tranquil ambiance that draws visitors year-round.

Murree is convenient, with well-maintained roads connecting it to major cities like Islamabad and Rawalpindi. The short distance from the capital city of Islamabad makes it easily accessible for a day trip or a weekend getaway.







Kalam

Kalam, located in the Swat Valley of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province, Pakistan, is a scenic and enchanting tourist destination. Nestled amidst lush green meadows, towering mountains, and gushing rivers, Kalam offers a serene and picturesque retreat for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

The Ushu River, which flows through Kalam, offers opportunities for trout fishing and leisurely walks along its banks. The region is also dotted with numerous waterfalls, including the famous Mahodand Waterfall, which cascades down the mountainside, creating a spectacular sight.

Kalam is accessible by road, with a scenic drive from Mingora, the largest city in Swat Valley. The journey to Kalam offers breathtaking views of the Swat River and the surrounding mountains, adding to the overall experience.







Neelum Valley

The Neelum valley is called the “PARADISE OF KASHMIR”. The valley is famous for its lush green meadows, pleasant weather, and freshwater streams. Dhani waterfall is the best tourist point in the Neelum valley. It is the biggest waterfall in the Neelum valley. It remains dry in the winter. Tourists can enjoy its beauty in summer. Kutton is also one of the most beautiful waterfalls in the Neelum valley. Athmuqam is the biggest shopping market in Neelum Valley, where tourists can buy traditional handicrafts.

One of the main attractions of Neelum Valley is its natural beauty. The valley is characterized by lush green meadows, dense forests, and cascading waterfalls. The Neelum River, with its turquoise waters, adds to the scenic charm, creating a serene and picturesque environment for visitors.

Neelum Valley provides an opportunity to immerse oneself in the beauty of Azad Jammu and Kashmir and create lasting memories of a remarkable journey.







Shogran

Shogran, located in the Kaghan Valley of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province, Pakistan, is a popular tourist destination known for its scenic beauty, lush green meadows, and panoramic views of the surrounding Himalayan peaks. It is situated at an elevation of around 2,362 meters (7,749 feet) above sea level, providing a refreshing escape from the heat and a serene atmosphere for travelers.

Shogran serves as a gateway to numerous scenic spots in the Kaghan Valley. Visitors can explore the nearby destinations like the Saif-ul-Malook Lake, known for its turquoise blue waters and breathtaking mountain backdrop. The lake is a popular attraction for boating and offers a picturesque setting for nature enthusiasts.

For adventure seekers, Shogran offers various activities. Visitors can enjoy horse riding through the meadows, go on hiking trails to explore the surrounding hills and forests, or embark on jeep safaris to visit higher-altitude destinations like Paye Meadows or Makra Peak.






Naran

Naran, located in the Kaghan Valley of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province, Pakistan, is a popular tourist destination known for its breathtaking natural beauty, p ristine lakes, and snow-capped peaks. Situated at an elevation of approximately 2,409 meters (7,904 feet) above sea level, Naran offers a picturesque retreat for travelers seeking tranquility and adventure.

Naran serves as a gateway to several spectacular lakes in the region. The Saif-ul-Malook Lake, situated at an altitude of 3,224 meters (10,578 feet), is a highlight of the area. Known for its crystal-clear blue waters, encircled by snow-capped peaks, the lake offers a mesmerizing setting for boating and photography.

Another nearby attraction is the Lulusar Lake, known for its pristine beauty and being the primary source of the Kunhar River. The lake is surrounded by towering mountains and offers a peaceful ambiance for visitors to relax and enjoy nature.







Skardu

Skardu, amidst the mighty Karakoram and Himalayan mountain ranges, is a captivating tourist destination in Pakistan. Known as the gateway to the world's highest peaks, including K2 and the surrounding Concordia region, Skardu offers a unique blend of natural beauty, adventure, and cultural richness. With its stunning landscapes, azure lakes, and ancient monasteries, Skardu leaves visitors spellbound.

The region offers challenging treks, such as the Baltoro Glacier and K2 Base Camp trek, attracting adventurers from around the world. The Deosai National Park, located near Skardu, is a high-altitude plateau known for its unique flora and fauna, providing opportunities for wildlife spotting and camping in a pristine alpine setting.

Another must-visit lake is the Upper Kachura Lake, also known as Shangrila Lake, famous for its surreal beauty and a small heart-shaped island within its waters.







Hunza

Hunza, situated in the Gilgit-Baltistan region of Pakistan, is a captivating destination known for its natural beauty and unique cultural heritage. Nestled in the mighty Karakoram mountain range, Hunza offers a blend of scenic vistas, historical sites, and warm hospitality, making it a popular choice for adventure and tourism.

Hunza is also renowned for its historical landmarks and ancient forts. The Baltit Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a prime example of the region's rich history and architectural splendor. Perched on a hilltop, the fort offers panoramic views of the valley and serves as a museum, displaying artifacts and exhibits that showcase the local culture and heritage.

The people of Hunza, known as Hunzakuts, are famous for their warm hospitality and friendly nature. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture by exploring traditional markets, sampling local cuisine, and interacting with the locals. The traditional music, dance, and festivals of Hunza add a vibrant and festive atmosphere to the experience.
