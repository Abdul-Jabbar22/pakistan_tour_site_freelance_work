Handicap

The Handicap service recognizes the unique challenges faced by disabled individuals when it comes to traveling. It acknowledges that disabled people may require additional assistance and support to ensure their comfort, safety, and enjoyment throughout their journey.

The provision of a personal carer is a key aspect of this service. The carer is specially trained to cater to the specific needs of disabled individuals, offering a high level of personalized care. They are knowledgeable about different disabilities and understand the various mobility aids, communication devices, and accommodations that may be necessary for the disabled person

The personal carer plays a crucial role in facilitating the disabled person's mobility during the trip. This includes helping them with tasks such as getting in and out of vehicles, using ramps or elevators, and maneuvering through different environments. They provide physical support, ensuring that the disabled person can navigate unfamiliar places with confidence and minimal hindrance.s



Moreover, the personal carer also provides emotional support throughout the journey. They understand the potential challenges and frustrations that disabled individuals may face, and they are trained to offer encouragement, reassurance, and empathy. This support helps to create a positive and empowering travel experience for the disabled person, reducing any anxiety or stress they may have.



The personal carer acts as a bridge between the disabled person and the various service providers involved in the trip. They communicate the disabled person's needs and preferences to ensure that appropriate accommodations and arrangements are made. For example, they may liaise with transportation companies to arrange accessible vehicles or communicate with hotels to ensure that rooms are equipped with necessary accessibility features.

Overall, the Handicap service and the provision of a personal carer aim to promote inclusivity and equal opportunities for disabled individuals in the realm of travel. By offering comprehensive support, this service helps to eliminate barriers and make it possible for disabled people to experience the joys of exploring new places, engaging in activities, and creating lasting memories, all while ensuring their comfort and well-being.
